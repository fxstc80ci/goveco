# Accountability Model

## Purpose

The accountability model records whether an entity that receives, controls, guarantees, or collects public-facing money is visible, auditable, and controllable by the public through direct or indirect mechanisms.

## Principle

No public burden without public accountability.

## Questions

For each entity, record:

- Who does the entity answer to?
- Is control direct, indirect, delegated, contracted, or unclear?
- How is the board or leadership selected?
- Is the entity subject to access-to-information rules?
- Is the entity audited by an Auditor General or only by an external auditor?
- Are compensation and procurement disclosures public?
- Are service standards and performance reports public?
- Can affected citizens appeal, complain, or challenge decisions?
- Are public funds, guarantees, rates, or mandatory charges involved?

## Key metric

`public_burden_without_direct_control` measures money or exposure connected to entities where citizens have no practical direct approval mechanism.
