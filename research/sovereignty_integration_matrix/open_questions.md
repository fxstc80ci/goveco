# Open Questions and Proof Targets

Status: working backlog  
Author: Craig MacPherson / Arti Muse  
Created: 2026-06-13

## Highest priority proof targets

1. Carney new-order rhetoric
   - Locate official transcripts and full context.
   - Separate geopolitical analysis from policy intent.
   - Capture exact quotes and dates.

2. Trump Canada pressure
   - Track tariffs, USMCA statements, annexation or 51st-state rhetoric, and formal trade actions separately.
   - Distinguish rhetoric from executive orders, tariff notices, and negotiation documents.

3. Emergency-style authority and major-project fast tracking
   - Identify bill text, statutory authority, cabinet power, regulatory bypasses, time limits, court review, provincial role, and Indigenous consultation rules.

4. Brookfield / Carney conflict-risk
   - Locate ethics screen terms, blind trust terms, recusals, parliamentary committee testimony, asset declarations, and Brookfield ownership exposure.
   - Separate direct conflict from sector-overlap risk.

5. Money flow to beneficiaries
   - Track procurement awards, grants, public-private partnerships, guarantees, loans, Crown corporation investments, and tax expenditures.
   - Map recipients to ownership and financing structures.

6. Media and narrative capacity
   - Track statutory funding, regulator powers, online harms legislation, platform takedown rules, misinformation authorities, and CBC governance.
   - Separate public broadcasting policy from censorship or narrative-control authority.

7. Defence and dual-use infrastructure
   - Track NORAD, NATO, Arctic, radar, drone, port, runway, telecom, cyber, satellite, shipbuilding, and logistics investments.
   - Identify which projects are military-only versus dual-use industrial corridors.

8. Resource security and export leverage
   - Track minerals, energy, water-related policy, agriculture, lumber, ports, pipelines, rail corridors, export controls, and trade disputes.

9. Constitutional and sovereignty impact
   - Track federal-provincial jurisdiction, Indigenous rights, treaty effects, emergency authorities, judicial review, and democratic oversight.

10. Timeline clustering
    - Determine whether major events cluster by month across rhetoric, trade pressure, defence spending, legal authority, media policy, and money flow.

## Research questions

- Is the evidence stronger for formal integration, informal dependency, elite capture, or normal crisis governance?
- Which policies create measurable public burden through taxes, debt, regulated rates, user fees, or guarantees?
- Which beneficiaries are direct recipients versus sector-level beneficiaries?
- Are federal powers expanding temporarily, permanently, or ambiguously?
- Are provincial, municipal, Indigenous, or citizen accountability mechanisms weakened?
- Does trade diversification reduce U.S. dependency or create a pretext for crisis-driven authority?
- Does defence integration create genuine security capacity or deeper operational dependency?
- Do media policy changes increase institutional resilience or state narrative capacity?
- Which claims are contradicted by documents?

## Completion criteria for first evidence pass

- At least 25 evidence rows.
- Each row has source title, URL, source type, confidence, and counterargument.
- No confidence score above 3 without primary or official evidence.
- Every money-flow claim has a source and amount basis.
- Every Brookfield relevance tag is supported by direct ownership, contract, financing, or sector-exposure evidence.
- Timeline includes enough rows to test clustering rather than assume it.
